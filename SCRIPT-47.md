## THE SCRIPT (47-slide order · [beat] = pause)

**[1-4 · cold open]** Don't speak. Let the film play. Start when the title card lands.

**[5 · Title card lands]** Right. That was a lot, wasn't it? Don't worry, I'm not going to talk to you like that for the next fifteen minutes. I'll let that one slip.

**[6 · The seed]** Quick backstory, so you know who to blame. I was ranting in the LinkedIn comments about how the Google algorithm has been fundamentally broken since the helpful content update in 2022. And I wrote one line: don't hate the players, hate the game.

**[7 · The invite]** Someone from EGR Global saw it and asked me if I wanted to write a piece. I said yes. I absolutely love to rant.

**[8 · The stage]** Then someone read that piece and asked me if I wanted to come and do it in real life. I also said yes. So welcome to Don't Hate the Player, Hate the Prompt. If this goes badly, it's a long chain of other people's decisions. I'm just the last link.

**[9 · The lie]** Here's the lie I'd like to kill in the next fifteen minutes. I'd apologise in advance, but I genuinely won't mean it. [hold a beat, then straight into the hands-up]

**[10 · Hands up]** Quick survey. Hands up, actually put your hand up so I can count, if in the last six months or so you've been told you need to start ranking in ChatGPT. [beat] Yeah. As expected. That's a lot of hands. By the end of the next fifteen minutes I want every hand in this room back down, and hopefully staying down.

**[11 · Who's selling it · posts wall (1 of 3)]** Put your hands down, because I want to show you who's actually doing the telling. These are real LinkedIn posts from the last twelve months. First up, Neil Patel, from NP Digital. Name and shame.

**[12 · Who's selling it · posts wall (2 of 3)]** Then you've got Steven. Julian. Lenny. All of them some version of how to rank number one in ChatGPT.

**[13 · Who's selling it · posts wall (3 of 3) → mirror]** And my favourite, on the right there, Connor. Which is perfect for the Terminator 2 analogy I'm running today. So thank you for that, Connor. That's six. I stopped at six because my thumbs started hurting. So before you buy what any of them are selling, do one thing for me.

**[14 · Mirror check (job titles)]** Take a look in the mirror. Because the person who told you to rank in ChatGPT probably had some kind of fluffy job title. Head of. Director of. Founder OR Growth? [beat] I'm not sure what that last one does all day, but he's very confident. And he's got a course.

**[15 · You don't rank. You surface.]** I'll say it early, so we know why we're here. Ranking in ChatGPT isn't a real thing. You don't rank, you surface. And the difference between those two words is the difference between a six-figure consultancy invoice and an in-house team that actually knows what they're doing.

**[16 · I'm Mark]** Hello, I'm Mark. I run SEO and ASO at MrQ. And the next fifteen minutes are probably going to upset some people. I think that's a really good thing. So let's go, shall we?

**[17 · GEO · AEO · LLMO]** Here's what happened. AI hit the mainstream, ChatGPT became a verb, and marketeers panicked. I say that with affection, I am one. And whenever we panic, a consultancy economy materialises overnight to sell the cure. It's got a dozen names: GEO, AEO, LLMO. Danny Sullivan coined LMNOPEO as a joke. They all end in E-O, engine optimisation, because the easiest way to sell something new is to make it sound like something you already buy. It's SEO with the price tag still on.

**[18 · 'AI SEO' isn't a new planet ★NEW]** So is any of this actually new? A sliver. Picture two circles, AI optimisation and SEO. They sell you AI SEO like it's a new planet you have to go and colonise. It isn't. It's mostly the same circle you already live on. There's a thin crescent that's genuinely new, and we'll get to it, but anyone telling you to abandon SEO and start again is selling a map to a planet that doesn't exist.

**[19 · 'Good SEO is good GEO.' Mostly. ★NEW]** And to be fair, credit where credit's due. Cyrus Shepard, who ran global SEO at Moz, mostly agrees: good SEO is good GEO. And he's mostly right. But mostly carries some weight, because it doesn't run in reverse. There are genuinely new things now: tracking whether you show up in the models, citation-gap analysis, making your site readable to agents, keeping your entities consistent. That's the crescent, it's real, it's just not a new planet. And take Google's advice with a grain of salt. They mostly want to sell us ads.

**[20 · Why is the model sceptical?]** But the cure is for an illness we don't actually have. So first: why would the model be sceptical of an optimised page in the first place? Because Google taught us this decades ago. Same reason you should never Google your symptoms.

**[21 · Consensus: a double edge]** If you type is the earth flat into Google, you get a confident no, it's roughly a sphere, and thank you Mark Williams-Cook for letting me steal that analogy. That's consensus working as intended. Now Google what's wrong with me next to your symptoms, and you'll be told you've got cancer and you're going to die. Same mechanism, opposite outcome. The site with the best SEO doesn't have the most accurate answer, it just has the best backlinks. It might be a content farm wearing a stethoscope. And ChatGPT is just querying Google's index via retrieval. Same source pool, same problem.

**[22 · We are the problem it was built to solve]** The model can't run a backlink audit, so how does it filter? By consensus. Six trusted sources saying the same thing about your brand outweigh one over-optimised page saying something different. Quick confession. When my girlfriend Googles something mid-argument to prove her point, I take the phone out of her hand and reverse-engineer the search to support my side, because the SERPs are downstream of how you ask the question. I've been doing this for ten years. She finds it annoying. It's completely fair. And I'm not the only SEO in this room who does that. The model knows people like us exist. That's why the system prompt reads the way it does. We're not its audience. We're the problem it was written to solve.

**[23 · 36% / 90% — Claude vs ChatGPT]** One caveat before the receipts: not every model behaves the same. New research from Profound's zero-click study shows Claude transposes Brave's top ten directly, the most ranking-like model on the market. But Claude's only about 36% of AI search. ChatGPT is a lot bigger. So when a consultant sells you ranking in ChatGPT, they're selling a behaviour that doesn't even exist in the engine that matters most.

**[24 · The receipts]** Right. That's the theory done. This is the bit where I stop having opinions and start showing you screenshots. Receipts.

**[25 · How it actually works]** When you ask ChatGPT a question, like what are the best online casinos in the UK, the model triggers a web search, pulls back a candidate pool, and runs it through a layer of instructions. That instruction layer is what I call the system prompt, and it tells the model what to trust and what to flag as suspect. That third box, the hidden one, is the bit nobody selling you GEO has ever mentioned.

**[26 · The leaked system prompt]** The system prompts for Claude Opus, GPT-5.5 and Gemini all leaked, and I covered them in EGR, which is why I'm here today. One line in Claude's prompt, and I'm not joking, should be tattooed on every iGaming SEO's chest: be appropriately sceptical of results for topics subject to a lot of search engine optimisation, like product recommendations, or results that might be highly ranked but inaccurate or misleading. Anthropic hard-coded that into Claude. The most optimised SERPs are the least trustworthy.

**[27 · You cannot out-SEO the system prompt]** So which SERPs do we think they had in mind? What query describes a product recommendation, highly ranked but possibly inaccurate, most precisely? Best online casinos. Best online slots. Best UK sportsbook. Every single high-intent commercial query in our vertical sits in the dead centre of the SERP this filter was written for. And you cannot out-SEO the system prompt. You cannot optimise your way past an instruction that names your entire category as content to distrust. This isn't a ranking problem, it's a source-quality problem.

**[28 · And it gets worse]** And it gets worse. In the newest leaked Claude prompt, Fable, product recommendations subject to a lot of SEO are filed alongside conspiracy theories, pseudoscience and contested political events. So your best online casinos page now sits in the same trust bucket as flat-earth content. That's the callback. That is the company you are keeping. Okay, the mic-drop bit is over.

**[29 · 69% — the Lily Ray study]** And if you're thinking, well, at least I get cited, research from Lily Ray ran a hundred best-category queries through Google's AI Overviews. Sixty-nine percent of self-promoting listicles got cited, and the brand that wrote them got left out of the actual recommendation. Citation is not recommendation. The competitors you named in your own listicle get recommended in your place. You wrote the guest list, and you didn't get in.

**[30 · Someone read ChatGPT's network traffic ★NEW]** One more receipt, my favourite, because someone did the unglamorous work. Suganthan Mohanadasan read two days of ChatGPT's actual network traffic. Three things. One, it fetches the web through commercial scrapers, so if your page isn't cleanly scrapable you're invisible in the tier that matters. Two, hide a fact behind JavaScript, like a price that loads on click, and it can't read it, so it quotes your competitor's number instead. Three, the killer, it will not cite you about you. The recommendation always comes from someone else. Third-party coverage wins.

**[31 · The framework]** So if you cannot rank in ChatGPT, what can you do? Here's the part nobody is selling, because there's no acronym to wrap it in yet. And no acronym means no invoice. You become a source the model wants to trust.

**[32 · Surface. Don't rank.]** Retrieval pulls a candidate pool, the system prompt filters it, and what survives is the small handful it trusts. So it's a trust problem, not a ranking problem. You win the trust filter, not the position.

**[33 · Ranking vs surfacing]** And I'll hold my hand up here, this is boring. There's no acronym, there's no thirty-day masterclass. It's a return to a set of disciplines the industry abandoned when programmatic SEO became cheaper than real journalists. Which is bad news for consultants, and good news for in-house teams.

**[34 · SEQ — the framework]** Let me show you the three pillars. I named it SEQ about a year ago when I started at MrQ, mainly as a joke. But then I went home and tested it, and it actually worked. That's why I'm standing here talking about it instead of in a meeting room making jokes about it. Structure, Expertise, Quality.

**[35 · S — Structure]** Structure is the boring stuff. Build the page so a machine can actually read it. Most casino sites bury the odds and payouts in prose it can't parse. It's the cost of entry, and without it nothing else matters, like the Metallica song. The model can't even read you. You're denied by the bouncer at the door, no matter how good your fake ID looks.

**[36 · E — Expertise]** Expertise is the bit consultants can't sell, because there's no shortcut. Real, named, qualified people writing your content, not content farms with rotating freelancers under pseudonyms. The model treats an anonymous page the way you'd treat an unsigned restaurant review on a domain you've never heard of. You don't buy it, you build it.

**[37 · Q — Quality]** Quality is where you accept the model has been instructed not to trust your domain pattern by default. You earn the trust back by being undeniably correct, undeniably useful, and undeniably original. Hopefully a bit like me today. Not rewritten from a competitor, not AI-generated. Original primary research, with numbers from your own platform, and player-facing utility the model can verify when it cross-references you.

**[38 · Machine true]** This is what Charles Floate called machine true. When six trusted sources say the same thing about your brand, you become machine true. It's entity stacking at work. It's also what Mark Williams-Cook has been saying publicly for ages. They're using different words for the same mechanism. I'm just yelling about it a bit louder today.

**[39 · OREO]** So the punchline's on me: it's just SEO. The same SEO that worked in 2014, before the industry ditched it for programmatic. Then my mate Dima sharpened it: this isn't even SEO, it's ORM, online reputation management, the thing we did back in 2010. He called it OREO. Probably a joke, but the joke's the answer, because the model is doing reputation management, just across six trusted sources instead of one review site.

**[40 · The work]** So after the framework, the logo and the three-letter acronym, the big secret is: do the work. I'm aware that's an anticlimax. I'm living it. [let it land]

**[41 · One.]** Let me land this in three sentences. One. Ranking in ChatGPT, that's not a thing, you surface in it. No consultant in the world can sell you a shortcut around an instruction in the model's own system prompt. Remember that.

**[42 · Two.]** Two. SEQ is just SEO. Structure, expertise, quality. The same three pillars, applied to a different surfacing mechanism. When you're in a rigged game for too long, you get sloppy and stupid.

**[43 · Three.]** Three. If you're about to sign a six-figure contract for LMNOPEO, take that money and hire some more people instead. They will move the needle further in twelve months than the consultancy ever will.

**[44 · Don't hate the player. Hate the prompt.]** This was Don't Hate the Player. Hate the prompt, and then go and build some content that the prompt was not written to distrust.

**[45 · I'm not here to sell. I'm here for the search.]** I was Mark. The entire talk this is based on is on EGR, and I'll be on the floor afterwards, come find me. Push back. Tell me I'm wrong, tell me I'm right. At least we got a story to share. Cheers.

**[46 · Don't take my word for it (sources) ★NEW]** And don't take my word for any of it. The leaked prompts, the Lily Ray study, Cyrus, Suganthan, it's all on screen now as QR codes. Scan it, go and check the receipts yourself, then come and find me.

**[47 · Outro (pre-recorded)]** [The outro plays: I am not John Connor. I never fought Skynet. I just figured out how to be cited by it, and so can you. Don't talk over it. Walk off on the music.]
